    • Madison Middle School

    When Maggie and I first met, we had no idea how crazy and amazing our first year of teaching would be.

    We met at Madison Middle School, where she taught 6th grade ELA, and I was the Computer's Class teacher.

    Madison was extremely tough!

    But we really found comfort in our friendship, which quickly turned into a blossoming courtship. There was something special about this midwestern gal.




    Golden Birthday

    Maggie and I planned our Spring Break to go to DC and visit her sisters. What I didn't know was that she had planned a "Golden Birthday" party for me!

    It was an amazing birthday week!

    We had a blast at Topgolf, played beer pong, and she reserved a dinner date at a speakeasy in DC.

    I have never had someone throw me such an amazing birthday. This was the week I knew I wanted to marry Maggie Thomas!




    "There's no place like HOME!

    After what appeared to be the longest school year ever, we paid out dues and were DONE with Madison Middle!

    We decided to pack our bags, and move to Kansas City!

    With a quick tap of her heels, Maggie and I were transported to KC, where she taught History at her high school, and I accepted a job teaching elementary Art & Spanish.

    Out of all my decision I've made in my life, following Maggie to KC has been the best of them ALL!




    WWI Memorial

    After spending an amazing school year in KC, I couldn't waste anymore time; I NEEDED to propose to Maggie!

    I coordinated with here family, and we spent the day doing her favorite activities:

    1. We went to church.
    2. Ate lunch at Q39.
    3. Went to the Nelson Atkins Museum with her family.
    4. Visited Old Red Bridge Love Locks to place out lock.
    5. Then we went to the WWI Memorial, where I hired a photographer to snap this photo!

    I threw her off with the Old Red Bridge Love Locks stop, and I am sooo fortunate that Maggie said YES!




    The Hobbs Buiding

    THIS WAS IT! OUR WEDDING DAY!

    And what a wedding day it was!

    Our ceremony was just perfect, and our reception was even better! Our wedding date was February 22, 2020, a few weeks before covid. We had no idea how special this night would really turnout to be.

    We practiced our first-dance routine for weeks, and all I can say about it was that all that practicing paid off!. The rest of the night was simply amazing, and I will never forget how to fortunate I am to have met my little KC-gal back in Nashville!
